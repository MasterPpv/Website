Hacking.
I'm trying to put together a hackerspace in the Southern Illinois area. In the spirit of Hacker Dojo and CrashSpace, we would like to offer an awesome venue where cool people can do cool things. The idea is to offer a social environment where people of all ages can come to network and improve their technical prowess. Although it is in the early stages, the idea is showing real potential. There is a lot of interest in the community for what we are trying to do, and many people are trying to help this get off the ground. To learn more about our vision, you can check out the website! crackerz.github.io/stlaurs_website

Research.
I'm involved in an organization known as the Saint Louis Area Undergraduate Research Symposium. We are part of a larger organization known as the American Undergraduate Research Society, who's goal is to organize high quality local research symposiums to undergraduate students. Undergraduate research is a relatively knew idea in academia, and I believe it has real potential to change the landscape of research. Getting students involved with research early in their career prepares them for what is to come. It is an investment in our future. It is this belief that drives our organization, the belief that students should be exposed to all aspects of research early and often. In that spirit, we offer students exposure to what takes place "behind the schenes" in organizing a research symposium. By hosting an entirely student run symposium, students see first hand the complexities of bringing academics together. To learn more about our organization, check out our website at stlaurs.com!
